# Assessment Evaluation

## Prompt / Input
**Grade Level:** 3rd  
**Standard:**  CCSS.Math.Content.3.G.A.1
Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.  
**Type of Assessment:** Multiple Choice

## AI-Generated Assessment

# 3rd Grade Shape Categories Assessment

## Questions

1. **What is a quadrilateral?**  
   a. A shape with 3 sides  
   b. A shape with 4 sides  
   c. A shape with 5 sides  
   d. A shape with 6 sides  

2. **Which of these shapes is NOT a quadrilateral?**  
   a. Rectangle  
   b. Rhombus  
   c. Triangle  
   d. Square  

3. **What do all rectangles have in common?**  
   a. All sides are the same length  
   b. 4 right angles  
   c. No parallel sides  
   d. 5 corners  

4. **Which shape is always a quadrilateral?**  
   a. Circle  
   b. Triangle  
   c. Pentagon  
   d. Rhombus  

5. **What makes a square different from other rectangles?**  
   a. A square has 5 sides  
   b. A square has 4 right angles  
   c. A square has all sides the same length  
   d. A square has no right angles  

6. **Which statement is true about a rhombus?**  
   a. A rhombus has 3 sides  
   b. A rhombus has all sides the same length  
   c. A rhombus always has exactly 1 right angle  
   d. A rhombus has no parallel sides  

7. **Which shape below is a quadrilateral but NOT a rectangle or rhombus?**  
   a. Square  
   b. Circle  
   c. Trapezoid  
   d. Triangle  

8. **What do squares, rectangles, and rhombuses all have in common?**  
   a. They all have exactly 3 sides  
   b. They all have the same size angles  
   c. They all have 4 sides  
   d. None of the above  

9. **Which of these is true about all quadrilaterals?**  
   a. All quadrilaterals have 4 equal sides  
   b. All quadrilaterals have 4 right angles  
   c. All quadrilaterals have 4 sides  
   d. All quadrilaterals have 2 sides  

10. **If a shape has 4 sides and 4 right angles, it must be a:**  
    a. Triangle  
    b. Rectangle  
    c. Pentagon  
    d. Hexagon
